About Me

This is what I do.

I have long loved numbers. As a school student I found math exciting. Math provided a language and framework to measure, design and understand the world.

As a professional engineer I worked on major projects, designing, building and commissioning process plants for the mining, oil, gas and coal sectors. Between designing plant layouts, sizing and selecting equipment, developing process flow diagrams into detailed plant designs, specifying equipment, instrumentation and control systems I saw the growth of process control systems monitoring thousands of data inputs.

With the development of powerful algorythms, the ease of collecting data on every aspect of equipment operation, increasing computing power and the falling costs of data analysis compared to capital equipment costs, the potential to optimise process plant operations via data analaysis has become a vital tool in not only understanding how the plant is operating, but a key tool to improving economic return and improving product quality. Technical analysis has evolved to Machine Learning, a powerful set of tools which enables problems previously unsolvable to be isolated, identified and solved.

This is my journey, to become an industry guru in Data science and analytics. My intent is to apply machine learning techniques to Real World Engineering problems. Follow me in exploring, understanding the issues and building solutions. The future is today.
